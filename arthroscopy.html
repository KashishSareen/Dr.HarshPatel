Understanding the Procedure

What is Arthroscopy?

Arthroscopy is a minimally invasive surgical procedure used to diagnose and treat joint problems. A small camera (arthroscope) is inserted through tiny incisions, allowing the surgeon to view and repair damage inside the joint.

Commonly performed on the knee, shoulder, and ankle, arthroscopy offers shorter hospital stays, less pain, and faster recovery compared to open surgery.

Minimally invasive — tiny incisions, less scarring
Less post-operative pain and swelling
Faster return to daily activities and sports

Why Choose Arthroscopy

Key Benefits

Arthroscopy offers distinct advantages over traditional open surgery for joint conditions.

🔬

Minimally Invasive

Tiny incisions (keyhole) mean less tissue damage, reduced scarring, and lower risk of infection.

⚡

Faster Recovery

Most patients return to normal activities within days to weeks, not months. Get back to work and sports sooner.

💊

Less Pain

Smaller incisions and less tissue disruption result in significantly less post-operative pain and discomfort.

🏥

Shorter Hospital Stay

Arthroscopy is often performed as a day-care procedure or with just an overnight stay, reducing healthcare costs.

🩺

Accurate Diagnosis

The arthroscope provides a clear view of the joint, allowing for precise diagnosis and targeted treatment of the problem.

🏃

Sports & Active Lifestyle

Ideal for athletes and active individuals who need to return to their sport or fitness routine as quickly as possible.

Is Arthroscopy Right For You?

Who Can Benefit?

Arthroscopy is ideal for patients with joint injuries or conditions that cause pain, swelling, and limited movement.

🦵

Knee Conditions

Meniscus tears, ligament injuries, cartilage damage, and loose bodies in the knee joint.

💪

Shoulder Problems

Rotator cuff tears, shoulder impingement, frozen shoulder, and recurrent dislocations.

🦶

Ankle & Other Joints

Ankle impingement, cartilage injuries, and joint conditions affecting the wrist, elbow, or hip.
