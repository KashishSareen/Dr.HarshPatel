Understanding PRP & Injections

What Are PRP & Injections?

PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma) therapy uses your body's own healing factors to repair damaged tissues. Steroid injections reduce inflammation, while viscosupplementation provides lubrication for arthritic joints — all without surgery.

Dr. Patel offers these non-surgical treatments to manage joint pain, reduce inflammation, and promote healing in conditions like osteoarthritis, tendinitis, and sports injuries.

PRP therapy for tissue repair and regeneration
Steroid injections for rapid inflammation relief
Viscosupplementation (hyaluronic acid) for joint lubrication
Non-surgical alternative to joint replacement

The Treatment Journey

How PRP & Injections Work

Step 01

Consultation & Evaluation

Dr. Patel evaluates your condition and discusses which injection therapy is best for your specific needs.

Step 02

Preparation

For PRP, a small blood sample is drawn and processed to concentrate the platelets. For other injections, the medication is prepared.

Step 03

Injection Procedure

The injection is precisely administered into the affected joint or soft tissue under sterile conditions. The procedure takes only minutes.

Step 04

Recovery & Follow-Up

Most patients experience relief within days. Dr. Patel will schedule follow-up visits to monitor progress and may recommend additional treatments.

Common Questions

PRP & Injections FAQs

PRP uses your own blood, processed to concentrate platelets and growth factors. When injected into damaged tissue, it stimulates healing and repair.

Steroid injections can provide relief for weeks to months. PRP and viscosupplementation results may last 6-12 months, depending on the condition.

There may be some discomfort during the injection, but Dr. Patel uses techniques to minimize pain. Most patients describe it as a brief pinch.

The number of treatments varies. Some patients need only one injection, while others may benefit from a series of 3-5 treatments for optimal results.
