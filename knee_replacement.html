Understanding the Procedure

What is Knee Replacement Surgery?

Knee replacement surgery (also called knee arthroplasty) is a procedure where the damaged surfaces of the knee joint are removed and replaced with artificial components — typically made of metal alloys, high-grade plastics, and polymers.

The goal is to relieve pain, correct deformity, and restore the natural alignment and function of the knee — allowing patients to walk, climb stairs, and perform daily activities comfortably.

Recommended for severe osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, or post-traumatic arthritis
One of the most common and successful surgeries in modern orthopedics
High-quality implants lasting 15–25 years with proper care
Conventional and minimally invasive approaches available

Types of Knee Replacement

Dr. Harsh Patel evaluates each patient individually to recommend the most suitable type of knee replacement surgery.

🦴

Total Knee Replacement (TKR)

The entire knee joint surface — femur, tibia, and patella — is replaced with prosthetic components. Ideal for severe arthritis affecting all three compartments of the knee.

  • Best for advanced osteoarthritis
  • Hospital stay: 3–5 days
  • Full recovery: 6–12 weeks
🎯

Partial Knee Replacement (PKR)

Only the damaged compartment of the knee is replaced, preserving healthy bone and tissue. This results in a more natural feel, faster recovery, and smaller incision.

  • For single-compartment arthritis
  • Smaller incision, less blood loss
  • Full recovery: 4–6 weeks
🤖

Robotic-Assisted Knee Replacement

Using robotic arm technology for sub-millimetre precision in bone resection and implant placement. Superior alignment, less soft-tissue trauma, and faster rehabilitation.

  • Sub-mm precision accuracy
  • Reduced blood loss & swelling
  • Faster functional recovery
🔄

Revision Knee Replacement

Performed when a previous knee replacement has failed or worn out. Dr. Harsh Patel has specialized training in complex revision surgeries requiring specialized implants.

  • For worn or failed implants
  • Complex, specialized procedure
  • FTJA fellowship trained
⚡

Minimally Invasive TKR

A less-invasive approach using a smaller incision (~10cm vs standard 20cm), causing less muscle disruption, reduced hospital stay, and quicker return to walking.

  • Smaller 8–10 cm incision
  • Shorter hospital stay
  • Less post-op pain
🦿

Bilateral Knee Replacement

Both knees replaced simultaneously or in staged procedures. Reduces overall recovery time and hospital visits for patients with bilateral severe arthritis.

  • Both knees in one session
  • Single anaesthesia exposure
  • Overall cost saving

Signs You May Need Knee Replacement

If any of these apply to you, schedule a consultation with Dr. Harsh Patel for a thorough evaluation.

😣

Persistent Knee Pain

Pain at rest or during night that doesn't respond to medications, injections, or physiotherapy after months of treatment.

🚶

Limited Mobility

Difficulty walking, climbing stairs, getting in or out of chairs, and pain that prevents normal daily activities.

🦵

Knee Deformity

Bowing in or out of the knee (varus or valgus deformity) that progresses over time and alters your gait.

💊

Failed Conservative Rx

Treatments like anti-inflammatories, steroid injections, PRP, bracing, and physiotherapy no longer provide relief.

🔴

Chronic Inflammation

Persistent swelling, warmth, and stiffness in the knee joint despite rest and anti-inflammatory medications.

😴

Pain Affecting Sleep

Knee pain severe enough to wake you at night or make sleeping in any position uncomfortable.

🏥

Severe Arthritis

Osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, or post-traumatic arthritis with significant cartilage loss visible on X-ray.

📉

Reduced Quality of Life

Inability to enjoy activities you love — social outings, travel, or family events — due to knee pain and limitation.

The Surgery Process

Step 01

Initial Consultation & Assessment

Dr. Patel reviews your medical history, conducts a physical exam, and orders X-rays or MRI to assess the extent of knee damage and determine suitability for surgery.

Step 02

Pre-operative Preparation

Blood tests, cardiac clearance, and pre-surgery physiotherapy. Our team guides you through what to expect before, during, and after the procedure.

Step 03

The Surgery (1.5–2 Hours)

Under spinal or general anaesthesia, Dr. Patel removes damaged cartilage and bone, then precisely positions the prosthetic components to restore natural knee mechanics.

Step 04

Post-op Hospital Care (3–5 Days)

You'll begin walking within 24 hours with physiotherapist guidance. Pain management and DVT prevention measures are actively monitored throughout your hospital stay.

Step 05

Rehabilitation & Recovery

A structured physiotherapy program over 6–12 weeks helps restore full knee strength and mobility. Most patients resume normal activities including walking and light exercise within 3 months.

Common Questions

Knee Replacement FAQs

There is no strict age limit. It's recommended based on severity of pain and disability rather than age alone. Patients aged 50–80 are most commonly treated, but younger patients with severe arthritis may also qualify. Dr. Patel evaluates each case individually.

Modern knee implants typically last 15–25 years with proper care. The longevity depends on your activity level, body weight, and the type of implant used. High-quality implants combined with Dr. Patel's precise surgical technique ensure maximum lifespan.

Robotic surgery offers sub-millimetre precision, better implant alignment, less soft-tissue trauma, and often faster recovery. It's particularly beneficial for complex cases. However, both conventional and robotic surgeries yield excellent outcomes in Dr. Patel's hands — the choice depends on your anatomy and specific needs.

Some pain and swelling in the first few weeks is normal and expected. Dr. Patel uses a multimodal pain management protocol — including nerve blocks, oral medications, and ice therapy — to keep discomfort minimal. Most patients find post-surgical pain manageable and far less than their pre-surgery arthritis pain.

Most patients can climb stairs within 4–6 weeks and drive within 6–8 weeks. Sitting cross-legged (Indian style) is possible for many patients — typically achievable within 3–6 months. Dr. Patel focuses on achieving maximum knee flexion to restore culturally important functional activities for Indian patients.
